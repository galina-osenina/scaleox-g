eCommerce Migration Solutions

Product and customer data

The first thing to do is to make sure all your customer data have been migrated to the new platform correctly.

Remember to make a plan of customer data migration.

Double-check all the customer order histories are ported accurately.

Migration process must account for all the contingencies and includes a customer communication strategy to set expectations.

Third party extensions

In the process of migration it is important to synchronize with a CRM system, email service, supply chain management software, or any other third party applications.

Also, there is a chance that user interface or workflow changes may occur during new eCommerce platform integration.

Make sure all your employees are trained and are aware of it.

Customer reviews and ratings

Reviews and ratings are the base of trustworthiness of your website. They are helping customers to choose what to purchase. Therefore, it is important to make sure every bit of data was imported to your new website.

SEO data

All marketing and advertising data is important to be migrated with customers and product data information.

URLs of product and category pages will help to make sure that the SEO value of your source website is not lost.

It will help you to increase ads indexes right after launches.

Integrations

Payment provider and order management system integrations, client and admin e-commerce tools, order management system, a payment solution are important integrations modules.

Remember to check all apps synchronize while replat forming.

Brand Image and Customer Experience

eCommerce website interface changes might create unpleasant deviations in the customers shopping experience.

Despite the fact that updating is always good, you may need to have gradual migration so your customers stay navigated and can easily make a purchase.

And remember to make an update announcement!
