eCommerce Migration Solutions

Product and customer data

The first and foremost is to make sure all your customer data migrated to the new platform correctly.

Don’t forget to make a plan of customer data migration.

Double-check all the customer order histories are ported accurately.

Migration process should account for all the contingencies and includes a customer communication strategy to set expectations.

Third party extensions

In the process of migration it’s important to sync with a CRM system, email service, supply chain management software, or your other third party applications.

Also there is a chance user interface or workflow changes that may occur during new eCommerce platform integration.

Make sure all your employees are trained and aware of it.

Customer reviews and ratings

Reviews and ratings are the base of trustworthiness of your website. They are helping customers to choose and make buying decisions. Therefore, it’s important to make sure every part of data was imported to your new website.

SEO data

All marketing and advertising data is important to be migrated with customers and product data information.

URLs of product and category pages will help to make sure that the SEO value of your source website is not lost.

It will help you to increase ads indexes right after launches.

Integrations

Payment provider and order management system integrations, client and admin e-commerce tools, order management system, a payment solution are important integrations modules.

Don’t forget to check all apps sync while replatforming.

Brand Image and Customer Experience

eCommerce website interface changes might create unpleasant deviations in the customers shopping experience.

Despite updating is always good, you may need to have gradual migration so your customers stay navigated and easily can make a purchase.

And don't forget to make an update announcement!
